What is Deep Sea Mining?

Deep sea mining is the process of retrieving minerals such as cobalt, nickel, and rare earth elements from the ocean floor. These materials are crucial for manufacturing electronics and green energy technologies like electric car batteries and wind turbines.

While it promises economic and technological benefits, the environmental costs can be enormous. Deep sea mining disrupts fragile ecosystems, stirs up toxic sediment plumes, and introduces harmful light and noise to the dark, quiet ocean depths.
