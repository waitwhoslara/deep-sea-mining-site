Who is Impacted by Deep Sea Mining?

Many countries are actively supporting deep sea mining, while others are opposed to it due to environmental concerns. Additionally, many are at risk geographically.

Countries in support of deep sea mining:

  • China: The global leader in deep-sea mineral exploration. China holds multiple contracts from the International Seabed Authority.
  • Russia: Actively exploring polymetallic nodules—valuable mineral deposits found on the ocean floor.
  • Japan & South Korea: Both have invested heavily in deep-sea mining technologies and maintain official exploration contracts.

Impacted / At-risk areas:

  • Pacific Island Nations: Including the Cook Islands, Palau, and Nauru—vulnerable to environmental damage from nearby exploration.
  • Papua New Guinea: The Solwara 1 mining project near the Bismarck Sea raised significant ecological concerns and was eventually canceled.

Opposed countries:

  • France: Supports a complete ban on deep sea mining.
  • Canada: Advocates for a 10–15 year moratorium.
  • Palau, Samoa, Fiji, and Micronesia: Have publicly called for an end to exploration and exploitation efforts.
