Who is Impacted by Deep Sea Mining?

Deep sea mining affects countries in different ways — some are actively exploring and supporting it, others are opposing it due to environmental concerns, and many are at risk geographically.

Countries involved in deep sea mining:

  • China: Holds multiple contracts from the International Seabed Authority and leads deep-sea mineral exploration.
  • Russia: Actively involved in exploring polymetallic nodules and cobalt-rich crusts.
  • Japan & South Korea: Both have invested in deep-sea mining tech and hold official exploration rights.

Countries at environmental risk:

  • Pacific Island Nations: Including the Cook Islands, Palau, and Nauru. These small nations face habitat disruption, sediment plumes, and threats to fisheries.
  • Papua New Guinea: The proposed Solwara 1 mining project near the Bismarck Sea raised major ecological and community concerns.

Countries calling for a moratorium or ban:

  • France: Officially supports a ban on deep-sea mining in international waters.
  • Canada: Advocates for a 10-15 year moratorium on deep-sea mining.
  • Other nations: Palau, Samoa, Fiji, and Micronesia have all called for a halt to exploration and development.

Major geographic regions targeted:

  • Clarion-Clipperton Zone (Pacific Ocean): A massive area targeted by companies for nodule mining.
  • Bismarck Sea: Coastal zone near PNG with proposed mining operations.
  • Mid-Atlantic Ridge & Indian Ocean: Other emerging locations being explored.
