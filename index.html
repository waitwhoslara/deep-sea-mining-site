Background

Deep sea mining is the retrieval of minerals from the ocean floor â€” including cobalt, nickel, and rare earth elements. These are used in batteries, electronics, and clean energy tech.

The process disturbs delicate marine ecosystems, stirs sediment plumes, and generates light and noise pollution that threatens undiscovered species in the darkest parts of the sea.

Countries Affected

  • Papua New Guinea: Proposed Solwara 1 project drew global protest due to ecological risk.
  • Japan: Ran experimental mining projects in 2020 to explore ocean-based resources.
  • Cook Islands: Exploring large-scale seabed mining licenses â€” sparking public concern.

Solutions & Conclusions

Environmental groups, Indigenous activists, and scientists are calling for a moratorium until we better understand the impact. Nations are pushing for treaties through the International Seabed Authority (ISA).

On an individual level: support sustainable tech, vote for climate-conscious policy, and spread awareness about deep ocean conservation.
